CMS Medicare Healthcare Claims Analysis
SQL · Python · BI
SYNTHETIC CMS MEDICARE-STYLE DATA · 1,000 RECORDS · 50 PROVIDERS · 2021–2023

End-to-end analysis of CMS Medicare-style claims data examining 30-day readmission rates, cost drivers by diagnosis category, provider type performance, and year-over-year payment trends. Includes Power BI and Tableau-ready exports.

Key Findings
  • Ohio had the highest 30-day readmission rate at 14.1% — 1.6 points above the national mean
  • Cardiovascular was the highest-cost diagnosis at ~$68K avg billed, with a significant Medicare reimbursement gap
  • Specialty hospitals had the highest readmission rate (13.2%); Critical Access the lowest (12.2%)
  • 85+ patients showed the highest readmission vulnerability across all age groups

FDA FAERS Adverse Event Safety Signal Analysis
Pharmacovigilance · PRR
SYNTHETIC FDA FAERS-STYLE DATA · 2,000 REPORTS · 4 DRUG CLASSES · PRR METHODOLOGY

Pharmacovigilance analysis of FAERS-style adverse event data using Proportional Reporting Ratio (PRR) disproportionality methodology to detect Pneumonitis safety signals in oncology drugs. Stratified serious AE rates by drug class, age group, sex, and year.

Key Findings
  • Oncology had the highest serious AE rate at ~51% — significantly above all other drug classes
  • Pneumonitis safety signal detected in oncology drug class (PRR range 1.6–2.9); 3 of 4 oncology drugs exceeded PRR ≥ 2.0 threshold
  • 75+ age group showed the highest serious AE vulnerability across both sexes
  • Oncology serious AE rates remained consistently elevated across all reporting years (2021–2023)
